Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a pioneering scientist who won Nobel Prizes in Physics and Chemistry.

Biography

Marie Curie was born in Warsaw, Poland, in 1867. She moved to Paris to pursue higher education at the University of Paris, where she became the first woman to earn a degree in physics.

In 1903, Curie shared the Nobel Prize in Physics with her husband, Pierre Curie, and Henri Becquerel for their work on radioactivity. Later, in 1911, she won a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry for discovering the elements radium and polonium.
